The Use of Cookies

A cookie is a tiny file that requires authorization to be put on the hard drive of your computer. Once you agree, the file will be added and the cookie will help analyze web traffic or inform you when you visit a specific site. Cookies enable web applications as an person to react to you. By collecting and remembering information about your preferences, the web application can tailor its operations to your needs, likes and dislikes.

To recognize which websites are being used, we use traffic log cookies. This enables us to analyze website traffic data and enhance our website to tailor it to the requirements of our customers. We use this information only for purposes of statistical analysis and then remove the data from the scheme.

Overall, cookies assist us to provide a better website for you by allowing us to monitor which websites you find helpful and which you do not. In no manner does a cookie allow us to access your desktop or any information about you other than the data that you choose to share with us.

Cookies can be accepted or declined. Most web browsers accept cookies automatically, but if you prefer, you can change your browser setting to decline cookies. This may prevent you from using the website to its full extent.
